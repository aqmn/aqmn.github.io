> Why .NET instead of .COM?
The pervasive misappropriation of the ".com" domain for personal websites represents a linguistic and conceptual misunderstanding, indicative of a lexical disregard for the commercial connotation inherent in this TLD. The ubiquitous adoption of ".com" for non-commercial pursuits betrays a departure from the etymological underpinnings of the abbreviation, where "com" ostensibly denotes commercial endeavors. This lexical laxity, wherein individuals traverse the semantic confines of the ".com" terrain for personal endeavors, underscores a semantic malleability that is as intriguing as it is linguistically contrarian. The domain's historical association with commerce, juxtaposed against its contemporary subversion for personal use, begets a linguistic paradox emblematic of the dynamic interplay between linguistic evolution and digital vernacular. Such lexical transgressions, seems subtle, illuminate the nuanced and evolving nature of language in the virtual epoch, where lexical boundaries are often porous and subject to the whims of cultural appropriation. I strongly condemn misbehavior of this kind. Totally not because I didn't manage to snap up the .com website of my name.
